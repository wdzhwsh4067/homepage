Teaching

I enjoy making machine learning and natural language processing approachable through hands-on examples and clear explanations. While I am early in my academic career, the cards below are representative sample courses that reflect the topics I would most like to teach and mentor in.

These are sample/representative course outlines, not currently scheduled classes.

Introduction to Machine Learning

Sample course · Undergraduate

Foundations of supervised and unsupervised learning: linear models, decision trees, neural networks, evaluation, and a capstone project that takes a dataset from raw data to a deployed model.

Natural Language Processing

Sample course · Graduate

From word embeddings to transformers and large language models: tokenization, attention, fine-tuning, retrieval-augmented generation, and the practical and ethical considerations of deploying LLMs.

Data Structures & Algorithms
